Transcript / Recording Script

This agreement is a simple three-party structure. The Buyer is buying the property as-is, meaning the known condition issues are already reflected in the deal. The Buyer gets a short review period to inspect and decide whether to move forward, but the inspection period is not meant to reopen the price or ask for repairs. The Buyer’s money goes through escrow so payoff, foreclosure-related amounts, and closing funds are handled with a clean paper trail. AeroVista’s occupancy is handled separately, so everyone understands who can stay, for how long, what payments apply, and what happens at move-out. High level: the Buyer gets a discounted investor opportunity, the Seller gets a cleaner path to resolve the foreclosure timeline, and AeroVista’s role is documented clearly instead of being left to side promises.

Investor Purchase Addendum — Clean Version

Purpose: Addendum to the purchase and sale agreement for an as-is, fast-close investor transaction with escrow-controlled payoff/reinstatement and separate post-closing occupancy terms.

1) Property Condition Acknowledgment

The improvements include a residence built circa 1927 with multiple additions over time. Seller discloses significant deferred maintenance and major structural/foundation concerns, including portions of the structure lacking a conventional foundation. Buyer acknowledges the purchase price reflects these facts and that Buyer is purchasing primarily for land/value opportunity.

2) Purchase Price / Funds

Purchase Price: THREE HUNDRED TWENTY THOUSAND DOLLARS ($320,000.00), reflecting the working final purchase concept after the $2,556.94 reimbursement adjustment. Buyer shall pay the purchase price in cash or immediately available funds through escrow.

3) As-Is, Where-Is; No Repairs; No Credits

Buyer purchases the property AS-IS, WHERE-IS, WITH ALL FAULTS. Seller makes no warranties. Seller shall not make repairs and shall not provide credits, allowances, or price reductions for property condition.

4) Due Diligence — Informational Only

Buyer may conduct inspections for informational purposes only within seventy-two (72) hours after mutual acceptance. Buyer may cancel by written notice before the period expires. Inspections shall not be used to request repairs, credits, allowances, or price reductions.

5) Proof of Funds

Buyer shall provide proof of funds satisfactory to Seller within twenty-four (24) hours of mutual acceptance and no later than 5:00 PM the same business day as acceptance, or the next business day if accepted after 5:00 PM.

6) Earnest Money

Buyer shall deposit $15,000.00 earnest money with Escrow Holder within twenty-four (24) hours of mutual acceptance. Earnest money becomes non-refundable and released to Seller upon expiration of the 72-hour informational period, except for an uncured title defect preventing marketable title or Seller default.

7) Closing / Trustee Sale Protection

The parties acknowledge a scheduled trustee sale on July 15, 2026. Buyer must fund escrow in sufficient time for Escrow Holder to remit any required reinstatement/payoff funds and obtain confirmation that the trustee sale will be canceled or postponed. Any extension requires written confirmation from Escrow Holder that the trustee sale is canceled or postponed such that Seller is protected.

8) Payoff / Reinstatement Through Escrow Only

Escrow Holder is instructed to order written payoff demands for all liens to be paid at closing, including deeds of trust, trustee/foreclosure fees, attorney fees, late charges, and any reinstatement amounts necessary to stop a scheduled trustee sale. Buyer authorizes Escrow Holder to pay those items from Buyer’s purchase funds through escrow. Buyer shall not make direct payments to any lender, trustee, or lienholder outside escrow unless required in writing by Escrow Holder and approved in writing by Seller.

9) Possession / Post-Closing Occupancy

Post-closing possession, if any, shall be governed by a separate Post-Closing Occupancy / Leaseback Agreement attached as Exhibit A and executed at closing.

10) Assignment

Buyer may not assign the agreement or addendum without Seller’s prior written consent, except to an entity controlled by Buyer solely for closing, provided Buyer remains liable for performance.

11) Conflicts

This addendum controls if it conflicts with the purchase agreement. All other agreement terms remain in effect.
