Simple Buyer-Facing Explanation

The agreement is set up so your initial payment of $17,376.60 covers the immediate reinstatement/cure amount and serves as nonrefundable consideration for the overall deal. From there, you would have the option to purchase the property at the agreed price, with the option staying available as long as the required payments and property-use obligations stay in good standing.

The monthly payment is set to match the current mortgage payment amount, so the property stays current during the option period. Seller keeps legal title until a valid option exercise and closing are completed. AeroVista would be the tenant/occupant side of the arrangement, but that does not give you ownership or possession before closing — your right is the purchase option, subject to the agreement terms.

The goal is to keep the property protected, keep payments current, avoid confusion about ownership before closing, and give you a clear path to purchase if everything performs as agreed.

Home Buyer shall pay Homeowner directly SEVENTEEN THOUSAND THREE HUNDRED SEVENTY-SIX AND 60/100 DOLLARS ($17,376.60) ("Initial Consideration / Reinstatement Support Payment") no later than May 11, 2026, unless another written payment deadline is signed by Homeowner.

Homeowner remains responsible for applying or directing the funds as Homeowner determines appropriate, including for reinstatement, arrearages, foreclosure prevention, carrying costs, taxes, insurance, repairs, or other Homeowner needs.

  • The Initial Consideration / Reinstatement Support Payment is nonrefundable except for Homeowner's uncured material default.
  • It is earned upon Homeowner's receipt.
  • Payment may be made directly to Homeowner. Homeowner shall provide a written receipt or other reasonable written confirmation of receipt upon request.
  • It does not exercise the Option and does not create any ownership interest, equitable title, lien, mortgage, deed of trust, tenancy-in-common interest, loan claim, or right to record an interest against the Property.

This amount is not a security deposit, rent credit, purchase-price installment, down payment, loan, or escrow deposit.

6. LATE FEES

If Monthly Rent is not received within five (5) calendar days of its due date, Homeowner may assess a $75.00 late fee. 7. OPTION TO PURCHASE

Homeowner grants Home Buyer an exclusive option ("Option") to purchase the Property, subject to strict compliance with this Agreement. AeroVista is not the option holder.

Home Buyer may exercise the Option only if:

  • AeroVista is not in default;
  • All sums due are paid in full;
  • No unauthorized transfer, lien, recording, sublease, assignment, alteration, or occupancy violation exists; and
  • Home Buyer strictly complies with all exercise requirements in this Agreement.

8. OPTION EXERCISE WINDOW

Home Buyer may exercise the Option only during Month 11 of the Initial Lease Term, beginning on the first day of Month 11 and ending at 5:00 p.m. local time on the last day of Month 11 ("Option Exercise Deadline"). 9. OPTION PURCHASE PRICE

If exercised during the Initial Lease Term, the purchase price is TWO HUNDRED EIGHTY-NINE THOUSAND DOLLARS ($289,000.00). The purchase price is not reduced by Monthly Rent, the Initial Consideration, late fees, repair amounts, utilities, or any other sums unless a signed written amendment expressly provides otherwise.

10. METHOD OF EXERCISE

Home Buyer must deliver all of the following to Homeowner on or before the Option Exercise Deadline:

  • Written notice of exercise;
  • Proof of funds or lender approval reasonably satisfactory to Homeowner;
  • Additional earnest money of FIVE THOUSAND DOLLARS ($5,000.00), to be deposited with escrow within two (2) business days after exercise and credited to Home Buyer only if closing occurs; and
  • Execution of necessary purchase documents and escrow instructions reasonably required to close.

Any non-compliant exercise is ineffective unless Homeowner waives defects in writing. Homeowner has no duty to remind Home Buyer of any deadline.

11. CLOSING AFTER EXERCISE

Closing shall occur no later than thirty (30) calendar days after valid exercise of the Option ("Outside Closing Date"), unless Homeowner agrees otherwise in a signed writing. If Home Buyer fails to close by the Outside Closing Date due to Home Buyer, AeroVista, lender delay, funding failure, or failure to satisfy buyer-side conditions, Homeowner may terminate the purchase rights and retain all sums permitted by law.

12. NO PRESENT OWNERSHIP INTEREST

Until closing is completed through escrow and a deed is recorded:

  • Homeowner retains full legal and equitable title.
  • No payment transfers title.
  • Neither Home Buyer nor AeroVista acquires any ownership interest.
  • No equitable conversion applies.
  • Neither Home Buyer nor AeroVista may represent that they own the Property.

12A. NO LOAN, PARTNERSHIP, AGENCY, OR FINANCING RELATIONSHIP

Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, agency, mortgage, deed of trust, financing arrangement, shared ownership relationship, or borrower/lender relationship among the parties. Home Buyer is a payment obligor and option holder only. AeroVista is the tenant and permitted occupant only. Homeowner remains owner unless and until a later purchase closing is completed through escrow and deed transfer is recorded.

13. OCCUPANCY, USE, AND AEROVISTA OCCUPANTS

Home Buyer has no right of possession or occupancy unless separately approved in a signed writing by Homeowner and AeroVista. AeroVista remains responsible for the conduct, guests, utilities, maintenance, insurance compliance, damage, personal property, and removal of all AeroVista Occupants and invitees. No nuisance, waste, unlawful activity, unauthorized transfer of possession, hazardous activity, or material change in use is permitted. Upon Homeowner's reasonable request, AeroVista shall provide a current list of regular residential occupants for notice, safety, insurance, and emergency-contact purposes. To the extent applicable non-waivable law treats any individual occupant as a tenant, subtenant, or occupant for notice or eviction purposes, the parties intend to preserve Homeowner's rights and remedies under Idaho law and all such persons shall be subject to this Agreement and any required notices.

14. ONE-TIME CONDITIONAL EXTENSION RIGHT

This Agreement does not automatically renew. Home Buyer may request one Conditional Extension only if all of the following occur:

  • Written extension request is delivered at least thirty (30) days before the Option Exercise Deadline;
  • Home Buyer and AeroVista are fully current and not in default;
  • Home Buyer pays a nonrefundable extension fee of TEN THOUSAND DOLLARS ($10,000.00), unless Homeowner agrees to a different amount in the signed extension addendum;
  • All parties sign a written extension addendum before the Option Exercise Deadline; and
  • No unauthorized lien, recording, assignment, sublease, alteration, or occupancy violation exists.

Extension economics, including rent increase, price increase, additional consideration, extension fee, deposit, insurance, and other terms, shall be negotiated at that time and shall not be effective unless agreed in a signed writing by all required parties.

If granted, the extension may last up to ninety (90) days and ends absolutely on the date stated in the signed extension addendum ("Final Extension Deadline"). No second extension exists unless Homeowner voluntarily agrees in a new signed writing. Failure to close by the Final Extension Deadline terminates all lease-extension and purchase rights.

15. NO SUBSTITUTE TENANT OR OCCUPANCY TRANSFER WITHOUT WRITTEN CONSENT

No substitute tenant, occupant transfer, sublease, assignment, delegation, or change in possession is allowed unless Homeowner first approves it in a signed writing. During an approved written extension, the parties may separately agree to a substitute or additional tenant/occupant, but no party has an automatic right to substitution. Nothing in this section permits forfeiture based on a manufactured, immaterial, or bad-faith default. Homeowner may enforce available remedies only in accordance with this Agreement and applicable law.

For non-monetary defaults, Homeowner may give written notice and a ten (10) day cure period unless the breach is incurable, illegal, threatens safety, materially damages the Property, clouds title, involves unauthorized transfer of possession, or occurs after the Option Exercise Deadline or Final Extension Deadline. No cure period applies after the Option Exercise Deadline or Final Extension Deadline unless Homeowner agrees in writing. Nothing in this Agreement waives any notice, cure, eviction, unlawful detainer, foreclosure, title, or other process required by applicable Idaho law, and nothing authorizes self-help lockout or removal where legal process is required.

If Home Buyer or AeroVista has an uncured material default when a cure right applies, or commits an incurable material default, the Option may terminate without refund to the maximum extent permitted by law. Option termination based on default must be based on a real and material breach and not on a manufactured, immaterial, or bad-faith default. Termination of the Option does not waive Homeowner's right to pursue unpaid rent, damages, possession, attorneys' fees, costs, or other lawful remedies.
